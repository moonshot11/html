Tour of Brolio Swolio's Resort and Sauna


This is Brolio Swolio. His sole belief is that leg day is for losers.




He has created a new attraction...




Brolio Swolio's Resort and Sauna




Straight ahead is Chateau d'Brolio...




...where you can enjoy fine dining by the fireplace.




Next up, the sauna!









While relaxing, guests can view the chickens freely roam the grounds




Adjacent to the sauna is an outdoor bar




Above the bar is one of the overnight suites




Each suite is decked out with castle decor




Across from the sauna is the observation tower




(At the foot of the tower is the other suite with an identical floorplan)




At the top of the tower is a beautiful vista of the surrounding grounds...




...with a view of someone else's base!




Below the sauna and restaurant is a door with a sign marked "employees only." I wonder what's through here




Looks like a series of maintenance corridors




Up the stairs is a...cramped detention cell?




Through the corridors to the right is a set of stairs




At the top of the stairs is an open corridor that leads to the manor behind the resort




The manor has a nice living area




Along with a door to the basement, and another to an outdoor patio




The basement has all of Brolio's basic crafting tables




The outdoor patio has a nice warm lounge area to enjoy the sun




Up another story is Brolio's bedroom and study









Naturally, he's got a large walk-in bathroom




Halfway up the stairs to the attic is a lounge with some leather furniture and beanbag chairs




The attic is...an attic




Back in the original maintenance corridor, there's another door to the left




Looks like a Pal is sitting all alone providing power to the whole facility...




There's a loft above the power station with a bunch of squalid straw beds. This must be where the chickens sleep.




Outside the living quarters is the corridor into the manor.




Beyond the sign are the chickens we saw earlier!




Here are the chickens. They seemed so happy from afar...




Opposite the chickens is a door that leads back into the original maintenance corridor.




That's the tour! It's based on my experience on a cruise ship, where the guest-facing stuff was so manicured and lavish, yet in stark contrast to the glimpses I had into the maintenance areas. It fascinated me how what I experienced was set up as a "stage," where the guests' curated experience hid everything needed for it to function.
